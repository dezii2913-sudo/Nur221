Epidermis — outermost, stratified squamous epithelium. 5 layers. No blood vessels. Houses keratinocytes, melanocytes, Langerhans, Merkel cells.
Dermis — middle layer. Connective tissue. Has blood vessels, nerves, hair follicles, glands. Collagen + elastin give strength.
Subcutaneous (hypodermis) — deepest. Mostly fat. Insulation, shock absorption, energy storage.

🛏️ Pressure Injury Staging

Stage 1 ▶
Non-blanchable erythema. Skin intact. Earliest stage — reversible.
Stage 2 ▶
Partial thickness. Pink/red wound bed. Looks like a blister or shallow ulcer.
Stage 3 ▶
Full thickness. Fat visible. NO bone, tendon, or muscle.
Stage 4 ▶
Full thickness + bone/tendon/muscle exposed. Highest infection risk.
Unstageable ▶
Wound bed covered by slough or eschar — can't see the depth.
Deep Tissue Injury ▶
Purple/maroon discoloration. Blood-filled blister. Can evolve fast.

Osteoblast — BUILDS new bone. Makes matrix + mineralizes. Thinks "B = Build."

💀 Osteoporosis vs. Osteomalacia vs. Osteomyelitis

Osteoporosis — "Porous bone" ▶

Decreased bone MASS. Mineralization is normal.

  • Risk: age, female, Caucasian/Asian, sedentary, smoking, low Ca/Vit D
  • Fractures: hip, vertebral (compression), Colles (wrist)
  • Dx: DEXA scan
Osteomalacia — "Soft bone" ▶

Normal bone mass, DECREASED mineralization.

  • Cause: Vitamin D deficiency (adults)
  • In kids = rickets
  • Sx: bone pain, muscle weakness, bowing
Osteomyelitis — "Bone infection" 🚨 ▶

Medical emergency. Most common cause: Staph aureus.

  • Risk: open fx, diabetes, prosthetics, IV drug use
  • Sx: local pain, redness, fever, ↑WBC, ↑ESR, ↑CRP
  • Tx: IV antibiotics 4–6 weeks ± surgical debridement

📉 Anemia Types — Sort by MCV

Microcytic (small RBCs) — Iron deficiency ▶
  • Cause: blood loss (heavy menses, GI bleed), poor intake, pregnancy
  • Signs: fatigue, pallor, spoon nails (koilonychia), pica
  • Labs: ↓Hgb, ↓MCV, ↓ferritin, ↑TIBC
Macrocytic (big RBCs) — B12 / Folate ▶
  • B12: neuro sx (paresthesias, ataxia). Pernicious = no intrinsic factor.
  • Folate: NO neuro sx. Common in alcoholism, pregnancy.
🧠 TipB12 = Brain symptoms. Folate = No brain symptoms.
Normocytic — Aplastic / Sickle ▶
  • Aplastic: bone marrow failure → pancytopenia
  • Sickle cell: genetic, Hgb S, vaso-occlusive crises, pain

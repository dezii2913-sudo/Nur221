🛡️ Chapter 11: Lymphatic

🔄 What It Does (4 Jobs)

  • Fluid balance — returns tissue fluid to blood
  • Immune defense — filters pathogens, makes immune cells
  • Fat absorption — picks up dietary fat in gut
  • Waste removal — clears cellular debris

🧬 Lymphoid Organs — Tap to Explore

Spleen
Thymus
Tonsils
Lymph Nodes
Bone Marrow
Spleen — LARGEST lymphoid organ. LUQ. Filters blood, removes old RBCs, makes lymphocytes. Post-splenectomy = ↑ infection risk → vaccinate!
Thymus — Primary lymphoid organ. T cells mature here. Active in childhood, shrinks after puberty.
Tonsils — Lymphoid ring in throat. First-line defense against inhaled/swallowed pathogens.
Lymph nodes — Filter lymph. Have B cells, T cells, macrophages. Lymphadenopathy = infection or cancer.
Bone marrow — Primary lymphoid organ. B cells mature here. Also makes all blood cells.

⚠️ Major Disorders

Lymphedema ▶

Swelling from lymph obstruction. Common post-mastectomy with node removal.

Treatment: Compression garments, manual lymphatic drainage, elevation.

🚨 NEVERTake BP, start IV, or do venipuncture on affected arm.
Hodgkin Lymphoma ▶
  • Reed-Sternberg cells (hallmark)
  • Painless cervical lymphadenopathy
  • B symptoms: fever, night sweats, weight loss
  • Spreads contiguously
  • Better prognosis, often curable
Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma ▶
  • NO Reed-Sternberg cells
  • Skip pattern spread
  • More aggressive subtypes exist
  • Risk ↑ with HIV, immunosuppression

🏗️ Heart Layers (outside → inside)

Pericardium
Epicardium
Myocardium

Pericardium — fibrous sac. Inflammation = pericarditis (chest pain + friction rub).
Epicardium — outer heart wall (visceral pericardium).
Myocardium — thick muscle layer. This is what dies in an MI.
Endocardium — inner lining + valves. Inflammation = endocarditis.

🫀 Cardiac Output

🧠 CO = HR × SV Normal CO = 4–8 L/min • SV = 60–100 mL • EF = 55–70%

Drop HR or drop SV → CO falls → tissues don't perfuse.

⚡ Dysrhythmias Quick Hits

Atrial Fib ▶
  • Irregularly irregular
  • NO P waves
  • Stroke risk (clots in atria)
  • Tx: rate control + anticoagulation
Heart Blocks ▶
  • 1st degree: PR > 0.20 sec, usually benign
  • 2nd I (Wenckebach): PR progressively longer → drop
  • 2nd II: Dropped beats without warning — DANGEROUS
  • 3rd (complete): Atria & ventricles independent — needs pacer
VFib / VTach 🚨 ▶
🚨 Code rhythms VFib = chaotic, no pulse → DEFIBRILLATE. Pulseless VTach → DEFIBRILLATE. VTach with pulse → cardiovert.

🧠 ABG Walkthrough — ROME

🧠 ROME
Respiratory Opposite
Metabolic Equal

Step 1: Is pH up or down? Alkalosis (>7.45) or acidosis (<7.35)?

Step 2: Does CO2 move OPPOSITE to pH? → respiratory

Step 3: Does HCO3 move SAME direction as pH? → metabolic

Step 4: Is the other value normal (uncompensated), partially compensated, or fully compensated?

🩸 Pulmonary Embolism

🧠 Virchow's Triad
Stasis • Hypercoagulability • Vessel injury

Presentation: Sudden dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, tachycardia, hypoxia

Dx: D-dimer, CT angiography, V/Q scan

Tx: Anticoagulation (heparin → warfarin or DOAC)

🚨 Respiratory Emergencies

Tension Pneumothorax ▶
🚨 Signs Trachea AWAY from affected side • Absent breath sounds • Hypotension • JVD

Tx: Immediate needle decompression (2nd ICS, midclav) → chest tube

Pulmonary Edema ▶
  • Usually from left HF
  • Pink frothy sputum, severe dyspnea, crackles
  • Tx: sit UP, O2, IV furosemide, morphine
ARDS ▶
  • Refractory hypoxemia (O2 doesn't fix it)
  • Bilateral infiltrates on CXR — "white-out lungs"
  • Tx: Mechanical ventilation, PEEP, prone positioning
